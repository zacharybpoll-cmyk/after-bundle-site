§ 01 · The two harms nobody warned you about

The GLP-1 works. The side effects are devastating.
AFTER. exists for the two that hurt you most.

Mechanism 01 · Ozempic face

Fat-compartment lipolysis and dermal-signaling change.

Fifty to one hundred millilitres of facial fat compartment volume disappears as the body draws down its stores. In parallel, GLP-1 receptor activation on dermal fibroblasts impairs collagen and elastin synthesis — independent of the weight loss itself. The pattern is distinct from age-related thinning, and it compresses into nine to sixteen weeks.

What you see in the mirror at month four: a hollowed mid-face, deepened nasolabial folds, a jawline that has softened, and the look of a face that has aged ten years in twelve weeks. Filler is the only intervention once the volume is gone.

Mechanism 02 · Up to 40% lean-mass loss

Rapid catabolic state, compounded by post-menopausal anabolic resistance.

Twenty-five to forty percent of every pound lost on a GLP-1 is lean mass (Karakasis 2024 meta-analysis). Baseline post-menopausal muscle loss already runs three to eight percent per decade; GLP-1 therapy layers additional acceleration on top. For a 58-year-old woman losing 20 kg, roughly five kg of muscle — five to seven years of normal decline compressed into twelve months.

The functional cost isn't abstract. The jar will not open. The stairs feel longer. The grocery bag is heavier. And the lower lean-mass quartile at 55 doubles your risk of a fall, a fracture, a walker, an assisted-living facility — years earlier than it had to.
